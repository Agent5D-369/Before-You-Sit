Red flags

When the sales energy is stronger than the safety energy

!
No clear plan if someone destabilizes
You hear lots of confidence and very little operational reality.
!
You feel rushed to trust faster than your body is ready for
Urgency and certainty are often used to bypass discernment.
!
Hard questions feel unwelcome
If asking about safety, aftercare, or roles makes the room tense, pay attention.
!
The guide feels spiritually absolute
Humility and clarity are safer than certainty and inflation.
Green flags

What a grounded, trustworthy space usually feels like

+
Clear role boundaries
You can tell who handles facilitation, support, and medical escalation.
+
Honest limits
People tell you what they do not do, not only what they can do.
+
Your body is not being argued with
Good spaces respect hesitation, pacing, and consent.
+
There is a real plan for hard moments
Not mystical confidence. Actual preparation.
Self-check

Use these questions before you commit.

Do I feel pressured to trust faster than my body is ready for?
If your nervous system says “slow down” and the space says “just surrender,” that is information.
Do I understand what happens if someone destabilizes?
If there is no clear answer, the room may be running on hope rather than readiness.
Can I ask hard questions without being shamed?
Healthy spaces do not punish discernment.
Do I know who is responsible if something goes wrong?
If accountability is blurry, risk is already hiding in the system.
